Maintain Bottle Temperature

Maintain Bottle Temperature

White wines and champagne need to be well chilled while red wines are served room temperature.

Provide the choice available

“Provide me with a choice available”

We offer a wide selection of wines to include both Old World and New World wines. Briefly describe e.g. name, country,  region, vintage/year and taste.

Present the bottle

White wines and champagne need to be well chilled while red wines are served room temperature.

Customer needs to see what they are getting. Present the bottle, hold the base with 1 hand, rest other hand on bottle shoulder.

Show the label.

Pouring Technique

Offer customer to taste their chosen wine before it is served. Allow them to look, smell and taste.

Do not over pour the glass

Remember that there should just be a small amount of wine in a glass, to allow the wine to breathe and the drinker to swirl and sniff.

If customer requests just a glass, pour to the grey dot (Cat 4 & 5) / leave a space the width of 3 fingers at the top of glass (Cat 1, 2 & 3).

Recommend Red Wine Carafe

“Recommend my red wine
to be served in a wine carafe”

Pouring a wine into a carafe causes the wine to aerate, softening the tannins and enhancing the fruit character. For older wines over 15 years of age, a carafe may be used for decantation purposes to rid the wine of sediment.

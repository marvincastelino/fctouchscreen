Wine Carafe

Overview

Overview

Our First Class service is out of this world, especially when it comes to food and wine. It’s a service we’re famous for, and attention to detail is a big part of our reputation. That’s why, in October 2016, we launched a new​ set of wine glasses on Cat 4 and 5 flights exclusively in First Class.

The new set included champagne flutes, wine glasses and a new red wine carafe.

Red Wine Carafe
The 250ml red wine carafe allows the decanted wine to breathe, enhances its flavours and also expresses a sense of generosity to the customers in terms of quantity and control over pouring their ow​n glass.

It is important to follow the subsequent guidelines in order to deliver a consistent service to our customers.

Introduction of Wine Carafe

Inflight Procedure:
After take-off – Purser Duties:

  • Offer a menu and wine list
  • If a wine list is accepted, let the customer know that red wine can be served in a wine carafe e.g.
“I’m pleased to inform you that on today’s flight we have the availability of the wine carafe for our red wines”


Note: The red wine carafe is not used during the Welcome Service.

Wine Carafe Request

  • Deliver a wine glass using a tray appropriate for the type of service
  • Present the requested red wine bottle
  • Ask the customer if they would like to taste the wine before its served
  • If a taste is accepted, pour a small amount of wine in the customers glass
  • Once the choice of wine is confirmed ask the customer if they would like the red wine to be served in a wine carafe
  • If declined, continue to pour the wine to the grey dot and replenish automatically
  • If accepted, do not continue to pour wine into the customers glass, excuse yourself from the customer and let them know you will be back shortly to deliver the red wine carafe
  • Return the bottle to the galley
  • In the galley, pour the wine into a wine carafe to just below the narrowest part of the wine carafe
  • Deliver the wine carafe and a folded paper napkin on a small lined silver tray
  • Pour wine into the glass to 1/3 of the glass
  • Put wine carafe at the back of the wine glass (no coaster required)
  • Put the folded napkin to the right of the carafe (folded edge next to carafe)
  • Leave using a polite service phrase

Pouring Technique

Pouring Technique

  • Hold the wine carafe base
  • The wine carafe should not touch the glass rim
  • Slowly pour until the glass is ⅓ full
Note: Wine is prepared to just below the narrowest point of the Carafe

Replenishment of the wine carafe

  • When the wine carafe is empty, ask if the customer would like to continue with the wine
  • If accepted, use a new wine carafe (prepare it in the galley)

Galley Management

Loading

  • 6 wine carafes in a drawer
  • Loaded in a non-chilled cart
  • Do not move the carafes to a chilled compartment​​​
Exchanged at outstations (i.e. not ROB)

After use

  • Rinse carafe
  • Wipe outside with a dry cloth
  • Return to dedicated drawer


Note: The wine carafes may be re-used during the flight.

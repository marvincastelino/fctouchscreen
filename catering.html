Catering Facts & Figures

  • At present on average we are catering approx. 178,000 meals a day , this varies depending on season and day

  • Number of Cart offloaded every day: Approx. 7500, There is a segregation in the ware wash between classes. It is important for crew to return items to the correct place or it adds additional work to the ware wash team as they need to sort before they can process

  • Number of people working to put together a meal tray:
    FC/JC one person as it is a cellular activity
    YC: 9-11 staff due to it being belt work

  • For a full cart it would take approx. 3.5 minutes to be emptied.
    Half cart approx. 2 mins to be emptied.
    It is important not to mix the items as each machine is set up in a particular way, i.e. FC/JC/JC/Hollow Ware

  • "Number of items that are washed per day :
    The team are processing between 2.5 to 3.5 million pieces/day. The variance depends on the return flight catered inbound pax load.
    It is important to return items in the correct manner or china will get damaged costing millions.

  • We currently have over 520 chefs with 57 nationalities

  • "Approx. 7 million fresh eggs and 700 tons of pasteurised liquid egg used per year. Picture shows the liquid egg as many staff seem to think we use powdered egg, which is something that we NEVER use"

  • Roster shift is 9 hours with one hour break, if required overtime is allowed maximum up to 11 hours with premium overtime payment

  • Approximately 80-85 carts are loaded onto a A380 full flight

  • Allocated time to offload used carts and reload new carts is about 35 mins
    If crew do not follow the correct Ready to Land procedures, this delays the turnaround of the flight, which in turn delays them in going to their next flight, this could accumulate and thus lead to a delay in a later scheduled departure

  • Emirates have 295 high loaders in Dubai.
    Depending on where the aircraft is parked it take approximately 20-25 mins for carts to be transferred from the aircraft to catering facility

  • When carts arrive all items must be manually removed from carts and canisters.
    Incorrect stacking of items can cause delays and also breakages which may also result in staff obtaining cuts to their hands.Inspite of the special gloves, sharp splinters still penetrate the gloves)

  • All melamine/crockery go through an industrial dish washer processing between 2.5 to 3.5 million pieces per day

  • The prepared food is stored in Chillers at the Airside logistics loading docks, and must reach there approx. 6-4 hours before a flight departs.
    This is one of many fridges that stores all the completed food items for all cabins & this is where our flight handler with conduct his checks.
    Temp is on average 2.5-3oC. Flight handler will wear special protective coats
